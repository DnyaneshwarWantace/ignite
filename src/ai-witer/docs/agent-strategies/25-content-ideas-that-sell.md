# Content Ideas that Sell - Complete Strategy Guide

## Overview
Content Ideas that Sell focuses on finding content ideas that attract the right type of customer - the one who buys. This framework helps you create content that educates, engages, and converts prospects into customers.

## Core Framework

### Content Idea Sources

#### Source 1: Customer Problems
**Method:** Identify problems your customers face

**Questions:**
- What problems do they have?
- What questions do they ask?
- What challenges do they face?
- What keeps them up at night?

**Content Ideas:**
- Problem-solving guides
- How-to content
- Troubleshooting articles
- Solution frameworks

#### Source 2: Customer Questions
**Method:** Collect frequently asked questions

**Questions:**
- What do they ask in sales calls?
- What questions come up in support?
- What do they search for?
- What confuses them?

**Content Ideas:**
- FAQ content
- Q&A articles
- Explanation guides
- Educational content

#### Source 3: Customer Objections
**Method:** Identify common objections

**Questions:**
- What objections do they raise?
- What concerns do they have?
- What prevents purchases?
- What barriers exist?

**Content Ideas:**
- Objection-handling content
- Comparison articles
- Proof/evidence content
- Risk-reversal content

#### Source 4: Customer Success Stories
**Method:** Document customer transformations

**Questions:**
- What results did they achieve?
- What problems did they solve?
- What transformation occurred?
- What journey did they take?

**Content Ideas:**
- Case studies
- Success stories
- Transformation stories
- Results showcases

#### Source 5: Industry Trends
**Method:** Monitor industry developments

**Questions:**
- What's changing in the industry?
- What trends are emerging?
- What's new?
- What's relevant?

**Content Ideas:**
- Trend analysis
- Industry insights
- Future predictions
- Market updates

## Content Idea Framework

### Framework 1: Problem → Solution → Result
**Structure:**
- Problem identification
- Solution explanation
- Result demonstration

**Example:**
"How to [Solve Problem] and [Achieve Result]"

### Framework 2: Before → After → Bridge
**Structure:**
- Before state (problem)
- After state (solution)
- Bridge (how to get there)

**Example:**
"From [Before] to [After]: The [Method] That Works"

### Framework 3: Mistake → Lesson → Action
**Structure:**
- Common mistake
- Lesson learned
- Action to take

**Example:**
"[Mistake] That Costs You [Cost] (And How to Fix It)"

### Framework 4: Question → Answer → Next Step
**Structure:**
- Provocative question
- Detailed answer
- Action to take

**Example:**
"[Question]? Here's the Answer (And What to Do Next)"
