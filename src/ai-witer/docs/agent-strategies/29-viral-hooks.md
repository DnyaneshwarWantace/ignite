# Viral Hooks - Complete Strategy Guide

## Overview
Viral Hooks create short hooks (up to 6 seconds) that capture attention and stop the scroll. These hooks are the foundation of viral content - if the hook doesn't work, nothing else matters. Great hooks create curiosity, surprise, or promise value immediately.

## Core Framework

### What Makes a Hook Viral

#### 1. Immediate Attention
- Stops the scroll
- Grabs attention instantly
- Creates curiosity
- Stands out

#### 2. Emotional Trigger
- Surprise
- Curiosity
- Fear
- Desire
- Shock

#### 3. Value Promise
- Clear benefit
- Specific outcome
- Immediate value
- Compelling promise

#### 4. Pattern Interrupt
- Breaks expectations
- Challenges norms
- Surprising statement
- Unexpected angle

## Hook Formulas

### Formula 1: The Surprising Statement
"[Surprising/Contrarian Statement]"

**Examples:**
- "Everything you know about marketing is wrong"
- "I made $1M by doing the opposite of what everyone says"
- "The secret to scaling isn't what you think"

**Why It Works:**
- Pattern interrupt
- Creates curiosity
- Challenges beliefs
- Stands out

### Formula 2: The Question Hook
"[Provocative Question]?"

**Examples:**
- "Why do 90% of businesses fail at scaling?"
- "What if everything you know is wrong?"
- "Are you making this $50K/month mistake?"

**Why It Works:**
- Engages mind
- Creates curiosity
- Prompts thinking
- Drives engagement

### Formula 3: The Statistic Hook
"[Surprising Stat] â†’ [Insight]"

**Examples:**
- "80% of founders work on the wrong things"
- "Most businesses lose $50K/month and don't know it"
- "Only 2% of businesses actually scale successfully"

**Why It Works:**
- Credible (numbers)
- Surprising
- Creates curiosity
- Builds authority

### Formula 4: The Story Hook
"I [Action] and [Surprising Result]"

**Examples:**
- "I quit my job and made $1M in 6 months"
- "I stopped working 80 hours and revenue grew 10X"
- "I fired my biggest client and business exploded"

**Why It Works:**
- Personal connection
- Surprising outcome
- Creates curiosity
- Relatable

### Formula 5: The Secret Hook
"The [Secret] [Audience] Don't Want You to Know"

**Examples:**
- "The secret to scaling that no one talks about"
- "What marketing agencies don't want you to know"
- "The hidden method that 10X'd my revenue"

**Why It Works:**
- Creates exclusivity
- Builds curiosity
- Promises value
- Stands out

### Formula 6: The Contrarian Hook
"Stop [Common Advice]. Do [Contrarian] Instead"

**Examples:**
- "Stop working harder. Work smarter instead"
- "Don't hire more people. Build better systems"
- "Forget more ads. Focus on better targeting"

**Why It Works:**
- Challenges norms
- Pattern interrupt
- Creates curiosity
- Positions uniquely

### Formula 7: The Transformation Hook
"From [Before] to [After] in [Time]"

**Examples:**
- "From $1M to $10M in 18 months"
- "From 80-hour weeks to 20-hour weeks"
- "From struggling to thriving in 90 days"

**Why It Works:**
- Shows possibility
- Creates desire
- Specific outcome
- Inspiring

### Formula 8: The Mistake Hook
"[Mistake] That Costs You [Cost]"

**Examples:**
- "This mistake costs you $50K/month"
- "The $1M mistake most founders make"
- "Why this costs you 20 hours/week"

**Why It Works:**
- Creates fear
- Builds urgency
- Specific cost
- Drives action

## Hook Structure

### Opening (First 2 Seconds)
**Purpose:** Grab attention immediately

**Elements:**
- Pattern interrupt
- Surprising statement
- Bold claim
- Provocative question

### Body (Seconds 2-4)
**Purpose:** Build curiosity and promise value

**Elements:**
- More information
- Context building
- Value promise
- Curiosity creation

### Closing (Seconds 4-6)
**Purpose:** Create desire and drive action

**Elements:**
- Benefit highlight
- Outcome promise
- CTA (optional)
- Engagement ask

## Hook Generation Process

### Step 1: Identify Core Message
- What's the main point?
- What's the key benefit?
- What's the transformation?
- What's surprising?

### Step 2: Choose Hook Type
- Which formula fits?
- What creates curiosity?
- What stands out?
- What drives engagement?

### Step 3: Write Hook
- Create opening
- Build curiosity
- Promise value
- Drive action

### Step 4: Test and Refine
- Test variations
- Measure engagement
- Refine based on data
- Optimize performance

## Best Practices

1. **Lead with Surprise** - First 2 seconds critical
2. **Create Curiosity** - Make them want to know
3. **Promise Value** - Clear benefit
4. **Be Specific** - Numbers, outcomes, timeframes
5. **Test Variations** - Find what works

## Common Mistakes

1. **Too Vague** - Doesn't grab attention
2. **No Curiosity** - Doesn't make them watch
3. **Too Long** - Loses attention
4. **No Value** - Doesn't promise benefit
5. **Generic** - Doesn't stand out
