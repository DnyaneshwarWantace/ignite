# Viral Ideas - Complete Strategy Guide

## Overview
Viral Ideas generates highly shareable ideas that can be used for content, ads, and more. These ideas tap into what makes content spread - emotion, value, surprise, and relatability. Great viral ideas are unexpected, valuable, and shareable.

## Core Framework

### What Makes Ideas Viral

#### 1. Emotional Resonance
- Triggers strong emotions
- Creates connection
- Inspires sharing
- Builds engagement

#### 2. Surprise Factor
- Unexpected
- Contrarian
- Reveals hidden truth
- Challenges beliefs

#### 3. High Value
- Provides real value
- Actionable insights
- Useful information
- Helpful content

#### 4. Shareability
- Easy to share
- Relatable
- Conversation starter
- Social currency

## Viral Idea Types

### Type 1: Contrarian Ideas
**Purpose:** Challenge conventional wisdom

**Structure:**
- Common belief
- Why it's wrong
- Real truth
- Proof/evidence

**Examples:**
- "Why working harder actually slows growth"
- "The real reason marketing doesn't work"
- "What everyone gets wrong about scaling"

### Type 2: Revelation Ideas
**Purpose:** Reveal hidden truths

**Structure:**
- Hidden truth
- Why it's hidden
- Why it matters
- How to use it

**Examples:**
- "The secret to scaling no one talks about"
- "What successful founders do differently"
- "The hidden cost of [common practice]"

### Type 3: Transformation Ideas
**Purpose:** Show what's possible

**Structure:**
- Before state
- Transformation method
- After state
- Proof/evidence

**Examples:**
- "How to 10X revenue without hiring"
- "From burnout to balance: The system"
- "The framework that changed everything"

### Type 4: Problem-Solution Ideas
**Purpose:** Solve common problems

**Structure:**
- Problem identification
- Why it exists
- Solution approach
- Results/outcomes

**Examples:**
- "Why you can't get qualified leads (and how to fix it)"
- "The real reason scaling fails (and the solution)"
- "What's killing your growth (and how to stop it)"

### Type 5: Comparison Ideas
**Purpose:** Show differences and better approaches

**Structure:**
- Common approach
- Why it fails
- Better approach
- Why it works

**Examples:**
- "Why [common method] doesn't work (and what does)"
- "[Common approach] vs. [Better approach]"
- "Stop [X]. Start [Y] instead"
